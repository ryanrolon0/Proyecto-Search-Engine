Service Man

 Jason Nicholls is a man in his late twenties who is going nowhere in life fast. He is a free spirit who has a very casual attitude toward things and has not been able to hold down a stable job or career. He is fired from his job for being continually late and unreliable and with the economy in recession, Jason knows that he has a slim chance of finding a new job. By a stroke of fate, while watching television at home, Jason considers an advertisement to join the Royal Air Force (RAF). After picturing a new career involving fast jets a smart uniform he is easily sold on the idea. Under the care and training of the RAF Jason becomes a better man. He looks smarter, walks taller and starts to develop pride in what he does including a love and passion for Mixed Martial Arts (MMA). However, Jason encounters several obstacles along the way while re-sitting a crucial examination where failure is not an option. In rival, Corporal Wright (RAF Regiment) he faces a showdown fight in the RAF Mixed Martial Arts (MMA) Charity Match while back at home, his mother is diagnosed with breast cancer. While a developing, love interest proves to be as much of a challenge for him as he stands at the crossroads of his life, he realises the importance of maintaining good relationships with friends and RAF training colleagues while focusing on completing the military training to become the promising serviceman he dreams about.
