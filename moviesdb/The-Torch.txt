The Torch

 Sixty-one high school seniors bear witness to the extraordinary first hand accounts of 13 Holocaust survivors. Never before had this many survivors accompanied a group of teenagers back to the camps. In a transforming journey of grief and exuberance, About legacy, and fortitude and mostly about humanity. There at the actual scenes of the crimes, the Nazi Death Camps of Poland, 61 high school seniors bear witness to the extraordinary first hand accounts of 13 Holocaust survivors. Never before had this many survivors accompanied a group of teenagers back to the camps. In a transforming journey of grief and exuberance, The Torch is a reaffirmation and celebration of a life nearly vanquished from existence. The Torch is passed from the survivors to the kids as a life-altering lesson of how we must always remember to remain human.
