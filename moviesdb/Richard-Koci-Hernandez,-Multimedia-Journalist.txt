Richard Koci Hernandez, Multimedia Journalist

 Richard Koci Hernandez is at the forefront of the next generation of journalism. At 12 years old, Koci began his obsession with photography and his love of visual storytelling with a trip to the Ansel Adams Gallery in Yosemite. 30 years, 3 newspapers and a national Emmy later, he finds himself teaching multimedia journalism at the UC Berkeley Graduate School of Journalism. In an academic environment typically reserved for doctorates, Koci reveals techniques that were conceived five minutes ago and will be outdated five minutes later. This engaging documentary profiles his fascinating journey from Yosemite to Berkeley.
