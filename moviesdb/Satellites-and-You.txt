Satellites and You

 Show opens on a team of horses hauling an enormous cannon up a mountain. The cannon crew is dressed in 17th century costume. They reach the top of the mountain, where Issac Newton introduces himself to the audience. He orders the crew to fire the cannon, to demonstrate how a satellite stays in orbit. Cut to Newton standing in front of the Space Shuttle. Newton goes on to explain the uses of satelittes, jumping cut to cut from Kennedy Space Center, to a satellite construction lab, to an apple orchard, to NBC Master Control, to a actual shuttle launch, to Paris,and finally, to the orbiting space station. There he confers with Albert Einstein, and bids goodbye to the Audience.
