The Doctors' Revolution

 A personal view, by a British doctor, of the excellent Cuban Health Service, its history, present state and its problems. Scenes of Cuba, a general practitioner and hospitals Interviews, in Cuba, with Fidel Castro, pioneers of the service and directors of the children's heart cent re and the vaccine institute. Interviews in England with an academic and a retired British government minister. The narrative follows the progress of a young boy through a hospital admission and a cardiac catheter operation, which is unsuccessful.
