The Whiskey Robber

 The young Attila Ambrus escapes the misery of his native Transylvania for the unforgiving world of post-communist Budapest. Struggling to make an honest living he soon falls into debt. With no other way to find the money, he decides to rob a bank. Then another, and another. And so begins the legendary string of 30 major bank robberies that soon makes him the anti-establishment hero of his day. Known only as 'The Whiskey Robber', his outlandish robberies, taunting of the police and daring escapes capture the public imagination. But his lavish lifestyle of fast cars, travel, alcohol and women begins to take its toll and after many successful years his luck finally catches up with him. His capture makes all the headlines but so does his brilliant escape from jail only a few weeks later. He decides to go on one final spree before retiring for good.
