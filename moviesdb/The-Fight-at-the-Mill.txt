The Fight at the Mill

 In this subject are shown the two opposites in typical bush men, one notable for brutality, the other endowed with he courtesies that distinguish the true man. The dramatic element running through the subject shows a born gambler and gentlemanly crook, who comes to the rescue of a maiden in distress. He accompanies her to a public dance. The crook's true character soon appears, and he meets with just punishment. In revenge, he visits an Indian camp, and bribes the natives to make a raid on the settlement. Their attack is made at an unexpected moment. A stand is made at the old windmill, and exciting scenes show the Indian attack and their final repulse. Finally, the gambler finds the settlement too warm for him, and he is glad to slink away to save himself from summary punishment.
