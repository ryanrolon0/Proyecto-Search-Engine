Three Friends

 Young Renault spends a year in isolation. Upon return, he seems completely degenerate. Upset by Renault's behavior, friends Roger and Romeo become his personal therapists. Using philosophy and imagination, Roger and Romeo attempt to restore their friend. However, they soon realize that everything they are doing seems to be motivated by external control. The three friends endure the stricture of narrative form and eventually rebel. The mystery lies in the controlling image.
