Through My Brother's Eyes

 The story of the close relationship between Charles Evers and Medgar Evers as told by Charles Evers. Of the seven Evers children, Charles and MEdgar had a special bond. Their parents were devoutly Christian. Their parents taught them that racism was wrong; for them the civil right movement was a Christian movement teaching love, liberation and equality for all. 'Through My Brother's Eyes' discusses how the brother's experiences inspired Charles to carry on his brother Medgar's for equality against the odds in Mississippi.
