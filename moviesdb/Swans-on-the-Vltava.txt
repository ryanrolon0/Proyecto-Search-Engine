Swans on the Vltava

 In 1918 the country of Czechoslovakia was formed in the aftermath of the First World War. The Munich Agreement of 1938 between Britain, France and Nazi Germany granted the Sudentenland, part of independent Czechoslovakia to Nazi Germany. In a move designed to bring peace in their lifetimes the British and French betrayed Czechoslovakian independence, leaving it isolated and vulnerable to Hitler, who was determined to crush the newly formed nation by force. However there were still some who held true to the dream of an independent Czechoslovakia. The influential Colonel Moravec tried to convince an unsuspecting parliament of the threat of Nazi Germany. No one listened to his warnings....
