The Ambassadors

 Every year, Israel unknowingly exports thousands of young ambassadors abroad who travel looking for their dream of making maximum money in a minimum amount of time - "hit the jackpot" - by doing aggressive sales jobs. The lies interlaced friction with the local population, and especially the immense pressure to chase the money, are causing the film's main characters to feel extreme emotions. How did these young street merchandisers become the true face of Israel to the world?
