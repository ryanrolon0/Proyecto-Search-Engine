The Real Thing

 Sandy's ambitions in the world of rock 'n' roll have been fueled by his love for the lifestyle. He likes the parties and the women and the drugs. Now he's getting greedy. A chance meeting with Jack Feral opens up new horizons. But is Jack all that he seems? Or is Sandy about to bite off far more than he can chew? Sandy Deans has never had to think of anyone but himself and he is not going to start now. He wants to be a rock star. But it is not just money and the adulation of fans that he desires. He likes the lifestyle of sex and drugs. Now he is getting greedy. At a late-night party he meets Jack Feral, obviously a successful investor, who boasts connections with the music business. Naturally Sandy wants to get to know this man. Sandy has what could be described as a night to remember with the beautiful Bernice, except that Sandy's memory is slightly impaired by drink and drugs. Sandy readily agrees to meet Jack in his pub to give him some cash for a quick investment in property. The door to the pub is opened early one morning by Steve the barman. Sandy has made sure he has arrived on time to impress Jack. When he enters the bar, Sandy's album is playing and his ego swells. But when he hands over the cash to Jack, things start to go wrong. Faced again with Bernice, Sandy has to try to remember his night with her. Is she Jack's girlfriend? Is Jack really on Sandy's side? What exactly has Sandy got himself involved with?
