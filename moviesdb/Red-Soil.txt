Red Soil

 Persistent foreign influences from the IMF in the late 1990s caused a major shift in the agricultural sector of some countries in Africa. Subsequently, many farmers turned to child labor for their farms to maximize profit especially in the cocoa and chocolate sector. children were trafficked and kidnapped and held as slaves on cocoa plantations to produce cocoa for the chocolate industry. A practice called "Modern day Slavery. It is on one of this cocoa plantations that Officer Francis Tompson, a plantation owner and Julia, a retired nursing officer put aside their differences to help rescue 200 child slaves trapped on the plantation in the time of a tribal conflict. Based on true events in West Africa
