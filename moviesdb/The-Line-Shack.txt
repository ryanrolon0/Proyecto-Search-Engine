The Line Shack

 The Line Shack is the first film written and directed by 70-year-old actor John Adrian Riley (SAG, AFTRA, AE). Set in the Old West in 1896, it is the story of a man who becomes a recluse after killing his best friend. Nat finds peace in the solitude of the line shack for 25 years until a stranger takes up work at the ranch, exposing the past and shattering Nat's tranquility forever. The film features outstanding performances by L.A. actor Jon Briddell, Criminal Minds, CSI Miami, Castle, Midnight Movie, and Ohio Theatre Hall of Fame member Phill Wilson, Teachers, Mischief. Filmed in the Midwest on a Canon XHA1 camera and edited on Final Cut Pro, the crew consisted mostly of high school digital media students.
