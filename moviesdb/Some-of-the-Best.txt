Some of the Best

 Host 'Lionel Barrymore' (qv) celebrates MGM Studio's twenty-fifth anniversary, introducing the vast array of movies and their characters from MGM produced movies. Although there are numerous movies from which to showcase, he introduces twenty-five for special presentation, one from each of the twenty-five years starting in 1924 and _The Big Parade (1925)_ (qv), and concluding in 1948 and _Easter Parade (1948)_ (qv). Barrymore also mentions what's in store from the studio movie-wise for 1949, with such titles as _The Stratton Story (1949)_ (qv), _The Great Sinner (1949)_ (qv), _Border Incident (1949)_ (qv), _Edward, My Son (1949)_ (qv), _The Red Danube (1949)_ (qv), _Scene of the Crime (1949)_ (qv), _Conspirator (1949)_ (qv), _Any Number Can Play (1949)_ (qv), _The Secret Garden (1949)_ (qv), _Intruder in the Dust (1949)_ (qv), _Madame Bovary (1949)_ (qv), _Malaya (1949)_ (qv), _Little Women (1949)_ (qv), _Take Me Out to the Ball Game (1949)_ (qv), _Challenge to Lassie (1949)_ (qv), _The Barkleys of Broadway (1949)_ (qv), _In the Good Old Summertime (1949)_ (qv), _Neptune's Daughter (1949)_ (qv), _That Forsyte Woman (1949)_ (qv), and _That Midnight Kiss (1949)_ (qv). He also shows behind the scenes footage of the studio's actual twenty-fifth birthday party.
