The Man with the Twisted Lip

 Nellie St. Clair becomes distraught over the disappearance of Neville, her respectable, middle-class husband and goes to Holmes and Watson for help. He was last seen by her in the second story window of a seedy waterfront opium den. When Holmes and the police arrive, they find a filthy beggar, not St. Clair, in the room. St. Clair's clothes are found in the room along with his son's broken toy and a bloody fingerprint on the window sill. Holmes initially suspects foul play. After St. Clair's coat filled with with copper coins is found on a nearby riverbank, the beggar in his cell and is able to solve the case and reunite Mrs. St. Clair with her husband.
